We plan to explore the relationship between COVID-19 death rates and vaccination rates across US states by visually examining their correlation. This analysis will involve gathering COVID-19 related data from the CDCâ€™s API and then extensively processing it to merge the various datasets. Since the population sizes of states vary significantly, we will focus on comparing rates rather than absolute numbers. To facilitate this, we will also source population data from the US Census to accurately calculate these rates.

For each state, I assume that the 2020 population is 100%, and calculate what percent is 2021 population value relative to 2020. Then I subtract the 2020 percentage from 2021 percentage. If the result is positive 3% - means that the population grew 3%, if it is negative 3% - means that the population reduced 3%.

# plot a bar plot of percent change in population by state
wider |>
  rename(year_2020 = "2020") |>
  rename(year_2021 = "2021") |>
  mutate(perc_change = ((year_2021 - year_2020) / year_2020 * 100)) |>
  mutate(state = reorder(state, perc_change)) |>
  ggplot(aes(state, perc_change)) +
  geom_col() +
